Lattice Optimization

Lattice structures enable the integration of multiple functional properties within a single design. By controlling material density, it is possible to tailor mechanical compliance and performance across domains, including structural behavior, heat transfer, and radiological response. Therefore, a systematic optimization approach is essential to design lattice structures that meet specific performance requirements.

About

I work across mechanical design, composite structures, CAD automation, and computational engineering, with a focus on building practical tools and efficient workflows that solve real engineering problems.

I bring over 10 years of experience in large-scale engineering organizations, primarily in the design of composite airframe structures. My work has covered the full product lifecycle—from requirements definition and concept design, through design for manufacturing, prototyping, validation, and into production and delivery.

In addition, I have experience in the HVAC industry, where I designed air-conditioning systems incorporating NBC filtration. Some of these systems were developed for high-volume production, with manufacturing rates exceeding 100,000 units.

Today, I am focused on advancing work in composite structures, structural optimization, lattice design, and the application of machine learning to mechanical geometries.

I am open to collaboration, consulting, and new opportunities — feel free to get in touch.
